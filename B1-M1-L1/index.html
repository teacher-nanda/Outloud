BEAT 1 · HOOK Same story, two tellers

One of these people you interrupt. One you let finish.

Read both. They contain exactly the same facts. Which one do you want to hear the end of?

Version A

I lost my passport in Rome. I went to the embassy. It was closed. I slept at the station. I got a new passport on Monday.

Five facts. Nine seconds. Nothing to picture.

Version B

So it was August, it was about forty degrees, and I was standing outside the embassy in Rome with a bag of melted chocolate — when I realized my passport wasn't in my pocket.

One sentence. You can see it. You want the rest.

💭 Tell me
  • Which version has more information? (the same — B just arranges it)
  • In B, what is happening in the background, and what is the event? (background: standing, heat, August. Event: realizing.)
  • Which word tells you the event is coming? (when)

Version B is not better English. It is better structure — and structure is what we build today.

BEAT 3 · BUILD Meaning

Background and event

A story needs two layers. English gives you a different tense for each one.

I was walking home when I saw her.

I start walking
I saw her
I get home

Past continuous = the long line (the background)  ·  Past simple = the dot (the event)

💭 Check your understanding
  • Did I finish walking home? (we don't know — the story interrupted it)
  • Which happened first — the walking or the seeing? (the walking started first)
  • Which one is short, and which one is long? (saw = short, was walking = long)
  • "I walked home when I saw her." Is that the same? (no — that means I walked home after seeing her)
BEAT 3 · BUILD Form

How it is built

was / were + verb + -ing
I / he / she / itwas working
you / we / theywere working
negativewasn't / weren't working

Two connectors do almost all the work:

when + short event  →  I was cooking when the phone rang.
while + long background  →  While I was cooking, the phone rang.

Same meaning, different order. While almost always takes the -ing verb.

BEAT 4 · PLAY Word Auction

💰 How sure are you, really?

You have 100 points. Read each sentence, choose Correct or Wrong, and bet the points you are willing to risk. Sure? Bet big. Guessing? Bet 5.

Your points100
Right = you win your bet. Wrong = you lose it. Confident and wrong is the most expensive thing in language learning.
I was reading when the lights went out.
While I walked to work, I was seeing an accident.
What were you doing at eight o'clock last night?
They were knowing the answer immediately.
She broke her arm while she was playing soccer.
When I was arriving, everybody was leaving.
1 Correct. Background + event.   2 Wrong — the tenses are swapped: While I was walking to work, I saw an accident.   3 Correct.   4 Wrong — know is a state verb, so it does not take the continuous: They knew the answer immediately.   5 Correct.   6 Wrong — arrive is a short event: When I arrived, everybody was leaving.
BEAT 4 · PLAY Story Cubes

🎲 Roll it. Tell it. Sixty seconds.

Roll the die. Whatever you land on, tell that story — and you must set the scene before you say what happened.

Start with: "So it was…" or "I was… when…"

⚀
A time you were somewhere you should not have been. A time technology failed you at the worst possible moment. A time you met somebody you did not expect to meet. A time you were completely lost. A time something went wrong with food. A time you were the only person who noticed something.
Teacher move: after they finish, ask one question — "What was the weather doing?" or "What were the other people doing?" Then have them tell it again with that detail included. The second telling is always better, and that is the point they need to feel.
