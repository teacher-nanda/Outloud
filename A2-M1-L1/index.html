BEAT 3 · BUILD Form

Two families of verbs. That is all.

Regular — just add -ed

travel → traveled  ·  stay → stayed
arrive → arrived  ·  ask → asked
book → booked  ·  wait → waited

Irregular — you memorize them

go → went  ·  take → took
lose → lost  ·  get → got
leave → left  ·  eat → ate

Did + you + go + ?

⚠️ The rule everybody breaks: after did or didn't, the verb goes back to normal.
❌ Did you went to Rome?  →  ✅ Did you go to Rome?
❌ I didn't went.  →  ✅ I didn't go.

💭 Check your understanding
  • "I went" — one verb or two? (one)
  • "Did you go?" — where is the past? (in "did", so "go" stays normal)
  • Can I say "Did you went?" (no — the past is already in "did")
BEAT 3 · BUILD Pronunciation — the -ed ending

One spelling. Three different sounds.

This is the thing almost nobody teaches — and it is why people ask you to repeat. Click each word and listen carefully to the very end.

1 · sounds like /t/

watched
worked
missed
booked

After a quiet sound: k, p, s, f, ch, sh

2 · sounds like /d/

played
stayed
arrived
traveled

After a noisy sound or a vowel

3 · adds a syllable /ɪd/

wanted
landed
waited
decided

Only after t or d

💡 You never have to think about this. Say the verb, then add the ending, and your mouth chooses the easy one automatically. The only one to watch is group 3 — because it adds a whole extra beat to the word.

Put the story in order. Type 1 to 6.

  Suddenly, the train stopped in the middle of nowhere.

  First, I booked a ticket to Prague.

  In the end, I arrived six hours late — but I arrived.

  Then I packed my bag and took a taxi to the station.

  Luckily, a woman next to me spoke English and explained.

  After that, I found my seat and fell asleep.
