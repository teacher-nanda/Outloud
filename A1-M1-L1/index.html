BEAT 3 · BUILD Meaning

One tiny word does an enormous job

In English you cannot say "I teacher." You must say "I am a teacher."

❌ I Brazilian.  →  ✅ I'm Brazilian.
❌ She doctor.  →  ✅ She's a doctor.
❌ We from Portugal.  →  ✅ We're from Portugal.

💭 Check your understanding
  • In "I'm a teacher" — am I talking about now, or about yesterday? (now)
  • Can I say "I teacher" in English? (no — never)
  • Do I need this little word in every sentence? (yes, when there is no other verb)
BEAT 3 · BUILD Form

All six, in one picture

I / you / he / she / we / they + am / are / is + the rest
I am→ I'm
you are→ you're
he is→ he's
she is→ she's
we are→ we're
they are→ they're

Three words do all the work: am (only with I), is (he, she, it), are (you, we, they). That is the whole rule.

To ask a question, swap the first two words:

You are from Brazil. → Are you from Brazil?
She is a doctor. → Is she a doctor?
